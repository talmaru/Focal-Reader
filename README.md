Focal Reader is a speed reading tool based on a technique called Rapid Serial Visual Presentation, or RSVP. The concept was first explored during World War II for aircraft recognition training. Instead of moving your eyes across a line of text, Focal Reader displays one word at a time in a fixed position.
A single letter in each word is highlighted in red. This is the Optimal Recognition Point — the position where the eye most efficiently processes the whole word without needing to scan it. Your eye parks at that fixed position and the words flow through it. The result is that the mechanical work of reading, moving your eyes across a line, is eliminated entirely. What remains is pure comprehension.
You can configure your reading speed in the Settings panel below. The ramp function lets you start slow and accelerate smoothly to your target speed, giving your brain time to shift gears. Sentence and paragraph pauses are also adjustable — these are the brief gaps after punctuation that help your brain process completed thoughts before moving on.
The full text panel shows your position as you read. Click any word to jump directly to that point. Use the paragraph and sentence buttons to navigate backwards.
