Reading Study

This study measures how reading speed and comprehension interact when using fixed-focus reading compared to normal reading.

You will read two short passages and answer questions about each. The process takes roughly five minutes.

Read at whatever pace feels natural to you. There is no penalty for taking your time.

No data is collected. Your results are calculated locally and never leave your browser. At the end you will see your own numbers and can choose to share them or not.

Phase 3 of 6 — Method Introduction
Fixed Visual Focus Reading

The next passage will be presented one word at a time using a technique called Rapid Serial Visual Presentation. A single letter in each word is highlighted — your eye should rest on that point and let the words come to you.

Your reading speed has been set based on your pace in phase one. The reader will start slower and accelerate to that speed over a few seconds.
